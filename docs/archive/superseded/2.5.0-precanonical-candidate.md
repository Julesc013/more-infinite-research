# Superseded Precanonical 2.5.0 Candidate

An internal MIR 2.5.0 candidate was built on 2026-07-12 and was never published. It used MIR 3.1.2 as its source anchor. The canonical `2.5.0` identity is reserved for the independent Factorio 2.0 backport that may begin only after MIR 3.2 is published. The former candidate's source commit, archive hash, structured-summary run ID, and superseded status remain authoritative in `.mir/branches.yml` under `mir_2_5_0.prior_candidate`.

The unpublished candidate:

- ported the then-accepted plan-first automatic compiler contracts to Factorio 2.0 while preserving published 2.4.0 technology IDs, settings, runtime namespaces, and save behavior;
- added RecipeFactV2, shared relationship indexes, schema-2 family rules, compatibility packs, coverage accounting, whole-plan safety checks, and deterministic exact-owner protections;
- applied target-declared Factorio 2.0 cuts for streams, effects, settings, recipe shapes, and emitters;
- repaired the Muluna and Astroponics prerequisite cycle, adopted iterative prerequisite checks, and retained strict generated-graph validation;
- split automatic-compiler action and research-creation controls, required separately reviewed data, and added conservative through custom policy expansions;
- added schema-2 automatic-family policy and CompilerProvider contracts with stable diagnostics;
- kept experimental assembler and lab families behind explicit opt-in, standardized automatic-recipe settings and locale text, and preserved old profiles through a hidden legacy bridge;
- published generation plans only after safety checks and retained the exact 2.3.5-to-2.4.0 settings and save-upgrade contract.

This candidate history is deliberately absent from the packaged `changelog.txt`: it was not player-visible release history and must not create a duplicate `2.5.0` header when the canonical backport is eventually released.
