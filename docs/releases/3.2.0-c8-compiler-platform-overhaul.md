## Implemented boundary

- The canonical `TechnologyCatalog` is schema 3 and is finalized after sanitation and graph proof.
- Rejected alternatives retain their exact designs and total hard-gate evidence.
- Catalog, GenerationPlan, and CompilationPlan projections are checked in both directions.
- `CompilerInput`, `CompilerResult`, and `EnvironmentIdentity` are immutable fingerprinted contracts.
- `compiler_orchestrator.lua` owns sequencing; the planner has zero imports from emission.
- `ProviderMetrics` records exact identity, semantic partition, environment, dispositions, depth, conflicts, timing, bytes, witnesses, provenance, and completeness.
- Ambiguous provider ownership and family attachment produce `REVIEW_REQUIRED` evidence instead of exceptions or guessed attachment.
- Researchability traversal is iterative and is fixture-checked with deep and wide graphs.
- Promotion governance is generated from `.mir/technology-governance.json`; shipped Lua contains no hand-maintained duplicate registry.
- Compatibility maturity, behavior, and scope are independent machine-readable dimensions.
- The schema-2 dependency matrix is exhaustive and permits no exceptions.
- Scenario evidence fingerprints resolve fixture mod IDs through repository `info.json` metadata, include the actual fixture source tree, and reject missing or ambiguous ownership.

## Fast validation contract

The rapid iteration gate is deliberately bounded to architecture, schema drift, contract coverage, exact dependencies, lifecycle tooling, documentation governance, deterministic packaging, and the focused compiler-contract Factorio scenario. It does not claim the later full ecosystem, performance, upgrade, human-review, or protected-release campaigns.

Before C8 can become release evidence, freeze the final assurance implementation and test catalog, materialize a fresh C8 plan, run all required no-reuse lanes, complete the human attestation, and create and independently verify a protected seal. No C7 capsule qualifies C8 package bytes.
