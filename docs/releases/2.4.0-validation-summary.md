# MIR 2.4.0 Validation Summary

## Current Result

MIR 2.4.0 has been reopened for a release-blocking native-owner settings repair. It remains the Factorio 2.0 port of released MIR 3.1.5, retains supported compiler contracts, omits Factorio 2.1-only technology effects and controls, and does not claim byte or feature parity with the modern line.

The native-owner implementation checkpoint is commit `d1ad340` on `tmp/2.0`. Focused Factorio 2.0.77 scenarios for default preservation, combined overrides, disabling, unsafe formula rejection, compiler contracts, and the prior adoption path passed during implementation. Those focused runs are development evidence, not a release seal.

The superseded pre-repair candidate at source commit `5963445b1b72efffc18b232f5367a7da640061e8` passed all 82 then-declared scenarios on Factorio 2.0.77. The structured validation run `29480972-467a-40ed-9a9e-5d11a52ac03c` and its seal remain historical evidence only: they do not qualify the package-visible repair for plastic, low-density structures, processing units, and rocket fuel.

The superseded archive was `dist/more-infinite-research_2.4.0.zip`:

- archive SHA-256: `5E1D16CEA5C8F43E8C257B73A3A5A728CE6DFB9ADA18B0CACDDB49DE3C5226E1`;
- package-content and package-source SHA-256: `6D0DED50982C9F7EDF339268BA9972D348F51B15DB566158C25E0DC043122EF9`;
- assurance semantic-content SHA-256: `88EEE8CE1CBB38C92C379B111B3C56921F22BBD97B2AC80AFE2B19456497BC8B`;
- size: 399,197 bytes; 166 entries; zero forbidden release entries.

Two independent builds produced identical archive bytes, but those bytes are no longer release candidates. The exact MIR 2.3.5 upgrade and release-targeted results remain useful regression baselines while the native-owner repair expands settings, balance, upgrade, and runtime coverage.

Canonical evidence is recorded in `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-assurance-qualification.json`, `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-rc-final-validation-summary.json`, `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-upgrade-proof-final.json`, and `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-release-targeted-final-summary.json`. Earlier candidate evidence remains historical and does not qualify these bytes.

## Target-Specific Boundaries

- Factorio 2.0 science-pack fixtures use the target's `tool` prototype representation.
- Recycler boundary recipes use the Factorio 2.0 singular `category` field.
- The two Factorio 2.1-only cargo modifiers and their unavailable controls are omitted or hidden.
- Automatic recipe-family creation remains disabled by default and requires reviewed providers or explicit experimental opt-in.
- Compatibility claims remain fixture- or named-load-evidence based; load success is not a broad behavior claim.

## Remaining Boundary

The prior seal at `.mir/evidence/candidate-seals/mir-2.4.0-factorio-2.0.json` is superseded and retained for audit. Implementation, expanded validation, a new exact archive, a new seal, and unchanged `legacy` promotion are pending. Interactive save/settings/technology review must be performed against the final sealed archive; the older screenshots do not qualify changed bytes. Resolving the pre-existing local `2.4.0` tag collision, tag creation, and publication are explicit maintainer gates and are not authorized by this backport run.
