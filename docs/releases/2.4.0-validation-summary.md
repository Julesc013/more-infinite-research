# MIR 2.4.0 Validation Summary

## Current Result

MIR 2.4.0 is runtime-qualified as the Factorio 2.0 port of released MIR 3.1.5. It retains supported compiler contracts, omits Factorio 2.1-only technology effects and controls, and does not claim byte or feature parity with the modern line.

The exact candidate at source commit `5963445b1b72efffc18b232f5367a7da640061e8` passed all 82 declared scenarios on Factorio 2.0.77. The structured validation run is `29480972-467a-40ed-9a9e-5d11a52ac03c`; every required group passed, including exact archive base and Space Age loads.

The release candidate is `dist/more-infinite-research_2.4.0.zip`:

- archive SHA-256: `5E1D16CEA5C8F43E8C257B73A3A5A728CE6DFB9ADA18B0CACDDB49DE3C5226E1`;
- package-content and package-source SHA-256: `6D0DED50982C9F7EDF339268BA9972D348F51B15DB566158C25E0DC043122EF9`;

Two independent builds produced identical archive bytes. The exact MIR 2.3.5 upgrade retained startup and runtime settings, technology level, fixture storage, and the scripted runtime effect. The release-targeted gate also passed its strict current-commit matrix, representative local Factorio 2.0 scenario, package rebuild, 466-row compatibility audit, and clean-tree check.

Canonical evidence is recorded in `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-assurance-qualification.json`, `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-rc-final-validation-summary.json`, `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-upgrade-proof-final.json`, and `.mir/evidence/2.4.0-release-targeted-final-summary.json`. Earlier candidate evidence remains historical and does not qualify these bytes.

## Target-Specific Boundaries

- Factorio 2.0 science-pack fixtures use the target's `tool` prototype representation.
- Recycler boundary recipes use the Factorio 2.0 singular `category` field.
- The two Factorio 2.1-only cargo modifiers and their unavailable controls are omitted or hidden.
- Automatic recipe-family creation remains disabled by default and requires reviewed providers or explicit experimental opt-in.
- Compatibility claims remain fixture- or named-load-evidence based; load success is not a broad behavior claim.

## Remaining Boundary

Interactive save/settings/technology review must be performed against the exact sealed archive; the older screenshots do not qualify changed bytes. Resolving the pre-existing local `2.4.0` tag collision, tag creation, and publication are explicit maintainer gates and are not authorized by this backport run.
