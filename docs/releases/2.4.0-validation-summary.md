# MIR 2.4.0 Validation Summary

## Current Result

MIR 2.4.0 is being rebuilt as the Factorio 2.0 port of released MIR 3.1.5. It retains supported compiler contracts, omits Factorio 2.1-only technology effects and controls, and does not claim byte or feature parity with the modern line.

The package-visible source has changed since the earlier 2.4 and internal 2.5 candidates. Their runtime results are historical evidence, not qualification for the new bytes. The active candidate remains unreleased until the following evidence is regenerated:

- static, architecture, compiler, settings, locale, balance, and package-hygiene gates;
- deterministic construction of `dist/more-infinite-research_2.4.0.zip`;
- the full target-declared Factorio 2.0.77 matrix, exact archive base and Space Age loads, and named ecosystem checks;
- exact MIR 2.3.5 upgrade with settings, research level, persisted state, and runtime-effect retention;
- content-addressed evidence capsules and a candidate seal bound to source, archive semantics, harness, catalog, target profile, and Factorio binary.

## Target-Specific Boundaries

- Factorio 2.0 science-pack fixtures use the target's `tool` prototype representation.
- Recycler boundary recipes use the Factorio 2.0 singular `category` field.
- The two Factorio 2.1-only cargo modifiers and their unavailable controls are omitted or hidden.
- Automatic recipe-family creation remains disabled by default and requires reviewed providers or explicit experimental opt-in.
- Compatibility claims remain fixture- or named-load-evidence based; load success is not a broad behavior claim.

## Remaining Boundary

Automated qualification, exact archive identity, and `legacy` promotion are pending in this worktree. Interactive save/settings/technology review, tag creation, and publication are explicit maintainer gates and are not authorized by this backport run.
