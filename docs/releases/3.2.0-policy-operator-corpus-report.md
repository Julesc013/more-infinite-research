# 3.2.0 Policy, Operator, And Review Corpus Report

## Outcome

MIR now composes compatibility overlays, active CompatibilityPacks, support claims, and source authorities through one context-owned, fingerprinted policy facade. Family resolution, generation authorization, science roles, and competing-productivity profile composition consume that facade rather than importing the pack registry directly.

All nine built-in family providers compose the schema-1 safe operator DSL. The central resolver delegates candidate enumeration, hard eligibility, effect selection, and grouping to the finite data-only operator library and no longer dispatches legacy selector, tier, or effect strategy branches.

Approved technology scope now requires a machine-readable `TechnologyApplicabilityEnvelope` with exact mods, bounded Factorio and feature scope, finite structural predicates, positive and negative examples, and a zero-or-explicit bound on newly matched content. `.mir/technology-review-corpus.json` begins the reusable reviewed corpus for mining-drill, assembling-machine, and logistics manufacturing families.

## Safety boundary

Policy composition cannot mutate prototypes. Applicability and examples never override hard prototype facts. A successful qualification or envelope match does not approve, promote, publish, or expand a compatibility claim. The reviewed corpus is training and review evidence for offline deterministic tooling only; runtime learning is not permitted.

## Validation

Static contracts verify context ownership, prevent planning consumers from bypassing the policy authority, enumerate every operator category, validate every corpus fixture reference and label polarity, and exercise deterministic approval-envelope transport. The Factorio compiler-contract fixture validates the operator registry, applicability fingerprint, reviewed-context match, fail-closed new-match bound, and composed policy snapshot. Output parity remains governed by the normalized 3.1.9 approved-delta campaign.
