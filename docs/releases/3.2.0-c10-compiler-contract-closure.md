## Closed Compiler Contracts

- Execution mode is explicit: `SAFE`, `PREVIEW`, `REVIEWED`, `STRICT_CI`, or `RELEASE`. Review-required candidates remain nonfatal and nonmaterializing in safe and preview loads.
- Schema-2 `TransformationPlan` is the sole authority for generated-technology creation and governed patching. Every operation binds its source candidate and alternative, exact precondition, expected projection, allowed delta, authority, and qualification.
- Schema-2 `MutationJournal` proves plan membership, exact before and after states, single execution, and completeness. Missing, duplicate, undeclared, out-of-plan, tampered, skipped, or failed work prevents an applied result.
- Immutable schema-3 `CompilerResult` is finalized only after execution, journal completion, output parity, graph parity, and sanitation parity.
- Schema-2 `CompilationSnapshot` captures normalized recipe, technology, item, entity, lab, science-pack, inventory, relationship, and graph facts with per-domain and top-level fingerprints. Qualification uses a compact delta and structurally shares unchanged domains.
- `CompilerContext.with_active` preserves nil-bearing multiple returns, restores nested contexts on all paths, and retains tracebacks. No public unscoped activation primitive remains.
- Finalization consumes captured `PolicySnapshot` authority and does not reread mutable live policy.
- Normal loads publish bounded compact catalog, plan, coverage, and evidence projections. Full catalogs are diagnostics, preview, validation, or offline-export artifacts.
- Hard byte budgets and compiler telemetry cover snapshot work, structural sharing, canonicalization, construction time, peak memory, total compiler time, and individual plus aggregate public artifacts. Missing measurements fail release-time performance checks.
- The offline PREVIEW exporter produces the exact full schema-3 catalog plus deterministic JSON and Markdown reviewer summaries.

## Technology And Recipe Inventory For Playtesting

The compiler closure deliberately adds no speculative broad gameplay scope.

- The 70 released baseline technology identities remain immutable.
- `recipe-prod-research_steel-1` is the one 3.2 stable-unreleased technology identity reserved for in-game review.
- `mir-auto-prod-manufacturing-assembling-machine-1` and `mir-auto-prod-manufacturing-lab-1` remain governed predeclared automatic-family identities from the 3.1 compiler line; they are not newly promoted by C10.
- Existing-stream provider work attaches eligible recipes and entity manufacture to established productivity owners without creating another technology identity.
- Candidate recipe membership is exact-mod-closure dependent. Safe and preview campaigns must review the exported full catalog before any candidate receives approval, applicability, promotion, or migration authority.
- Recovery, recycling, catalyst, self-return, voiding, reverse-conversion, ambiguous multi-output, and unbounded progression families remain rejected or review-required rather than automatically promoted.

Playtesting should therefore focus on the steel technology, existing owner attachments, the two governed automatic-family identities where explicitly enabled, technology-tree presentation, settings transitions, save upgrade/removal behavior, and exact third-party profile output. Any later accepted tuning or promotion that changes package bytes creates a new candidate.

## Supersession Finding

The no-reuse C10 full plan passed 18 fresh lanes, including both exact-ZIP loads, before `scenario/2.1/semantic-family-attach` found that the exact singleton `pack-only-recipe` seed was incorrectly treated as ambiguous. The failure was in packaged compiler discovery logic, so C10 could not remain the current candidate. C11 corrects that defect and is the authority for further qualification.
