# MIR 2.3.5 Backport Plan

MIR 2.3.5 is the semantic Factorio 2.0 port of the immutable 3.0.5 behavior baseline. It is not an independent architecture branch.

## Contract

```text
2.3.5 = released 3.0.5 behavior
      + Factorio 2.0 target profile
      + retained proven 2.3.0 adaptations
      - unsupported Factorio 2.1 surfaces
```

## Completed Work

- The final expanded settings, recycler return, inverse cap, scoped cap, negative speed floor, unrestricted module, effect scaling, ownership, and prerequisite contracts were ported.
- Factorio 2.1 cargo modifiers and dependency floors remain excluded.
- The stale RC4 identity is preserved as superseded evidence.
- The final source and archive are frozen at `861565d` and `97B3DC9B...68DE`.
- All 71 expected Factorio 2.0 scenarios passed.
- The clean release-targeted gate passed against the local 2.0 library.
- The exact distribution loaded in base and official Space Age configurations.
- An exact 2.3.0 save with a Factorio 2.0 settings record loaded under exact 2.3.5.

## Remaining Work

The only release blocker is interactive settings UI and retained-value inspection. After acceptance, fast-forward or merge the exact candidate to `legacy`, rerun freshness without rebuilding, tag, upload the recorded archive, verify Factorio 2.0 visibility, and mark the release published/frozen in a repository-only evidence commit.
