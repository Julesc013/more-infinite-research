# MIR 3.2.0 C15 Local Qualification Report

C15 is not release-ready. Its exact no-reuse paired performance campaign completed all 72 governed Factorio invocations and found a reproducible compiler planning regression. The medium BZ plus Space Age and large K2SO lanes passed, but Base, Space Age, diagnostics-off, diagnostics-on, and four derived compiler phases failed their predeclared budgets. Manual review, protected qualification, sealing, and publication remain blocked.

This is a failed qualification record, not accepted release evidence. The compact raw result is `.mir/evidence/3.2.0-c15-performance-campaign-failure.json`; its status is `failed` and it cannot satisfy `runtime.performance-regression`.

## Historical-Claim Separation

The exact K2SO lane proves the corrected harness boundary:

- every 3.1.9 baseline process loaded successfully;
- the baseline did not satisfy C15's newer five-pack science claim;
- every C15 process and current science/sanitation claim passed;
- the lane remained performance-neutral at `+0.276708%`.

The baseline is therefore valid as historical load and timing evidence without being misrepresented as proof of a behavioral contract added by C15.

## Root-Cause Judgment

The evidence supports a fixed small-profile compiler cost dominated by repeated semantic validation and refingerprinting across design, candidate, qualification, catalog, compiler-result, and output-validation boundaries.

This is not a broad ecosystem scaling regression:

- the medium ecosystem was 14.22 percent faster than 3.1.9;
- the large K2SO ecosystem was performance-neutral;
- every candidate load and current K2SO claim passed;
- all artifact-volume and memory bounds passed.

The profiler also shows why merely raising a timing ceiling would be wrong. The Base catalog contains 73 candidates and 146 alternatives, yet validation performs tens of thousands of fingerprints and repeatedly canonicalizes multi-megabyte structures. That work must be reduced while preserving fail-closed trust-boundary validation.

## Required Remediation

Any correction to packaged compiler Lua creates C16. The next package candidate should:

1. validate immutable designs and gates once when they enter a trust boundary;
2. carry an explicit validated/trusted state through internal catalog stages;
3. avoid recomputing evidence fingerprints during every nested validation pass;
4. compare stored semantic fingerprints where both operands are already validated;
5. avoid full multi-megabyte catalog canonicalization except at declared authority boundaries;
6. preserve external-input validation, mutation detection, deterministic output, diagnostics, and exact public fingerprints;
7. add narrow call-count or work-volume regression tests before rerunning performance;
8. repeat the complete paired campaign against 3.1.9 without weakening any budget.

C15 remains useful for manual gameplay inspection, but it must not be sealed or published. Do not dispatch protected qualification while this exact automated blocker remains.
