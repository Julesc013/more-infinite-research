# MIR 3.2.0 C15 In-Game Playtest Guide

This is the practical order for tonight's C15 gameplay, compatibility, and tuning session. Use the exact ZIP below throughout the session. If a package-visible fix is made, stop calling the result C15 and rebuild under a later candidate identity before collecting more candidate-bound observations.

## What Is Actually New Since 3.1.9

There is one newly shipped stable research identity, not a large hidden batch of automatically generated technologies:

| Environment | Steel ownership to expect | What to inspect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Base game | MIR `recipe-prod-research_steel-1` | Steel recipes, prerequisite position, science packs, cost curve, icon, name, and infinite continuation |
| Space Age | Vanilla `steel-plate-productivity` remains the single owner | Steel smelting and casting effects are attached without a duplicate MIR steel technology |

Automatic assembler and lab family creation remains experimental and is not newly enabled by default. Diagnostic candidates for broader chemistry, biological, recycling, recovery, matter, and catalyst families are not promoted technologies. If one appears without an explicit experimental setting, record it as `unexpected-technology`.

The important changed behavior to inspect is compiler correctness around the established stream set:

- K2SO science and lab progression is observed only after K2SO's final data rewrites;
- impossible recipe, item, quality, location, ammunition, and entity effects are removed before final assertions;
- valid planet discovery and different-quality item grants are retained;
- low-density structures, plastic, processing units, rocket fuel, and Space Age steel use native-owner binding without duplicate technologies;
- existing MIR IDs, settings, migrations, runtime state, and established generated streams remain stable;
- Factorio 2.1.11 is now the minimum supported 2.1 runtime.

## Recommended Session Order

### 1. Base-only control

Start a clean base game with only MIR enabled. Confirm:

- `recipe-prod-research_steel-1` exists exactly once;
- its first level is placed after a sensible finite prerequisite;
- every effect names a real steel recipe;
- the first-level cost, time, science, percentage, and growth formula feel proportionate to other productivity streams;
- established research did not disappear, duplicate, move to an absurd tier, or acquire unrelated recipes;
- icons, descriptions, settings, research queue, save, reload, and partial research behave normally.

Then load a copied 3.1.9 save. Check researched levels, current research, fractional progress, settings, and generated technology ownership before and after saving again under C15.

### 2. Space Age ownership and late progression

Enable the official Space Age bundle. Confirm:

- vanilla `steel-plate-productivity` is the only steel owner;
- steel smelting and casting recipes are covered once;
- low-density structures, plastic, processing units, and rocket fuel have one owner each;
- Vulcanus, Gleba, Fulgora, Aquilo, and space-platform technologies use plausible prerequisites and science;
- electromagnetic, agricultural, metallurgic, cryogenic, and promethium science appear only where progression makes sense;
- native-owner arrows, icons, locale, costs, effect values, and infinite continuation remain readable.

### 3. K2SO regression and tuning pass

Run `Krastorio2-spaced-out` plus `xy-k2so-enhancements-nulls-fork` with Space Age. This is the highest-priority C15-specific pass.

Open `recipe-prod-research_advanced_circuit-1`. It must include all of:

- automation science;
- logistic science;
- chemical science;
- production science;
- electromagnetic science.

It must not show red science alone. Sample several other generated streams at early, middle, and late progression. Verify that each uses packs accepted by an available lab, has reachable prerequisites, and appears at a sensible point in K2SO progression. Record any missing green, blue, purple, space, planet, or K2-specific science as `wrong-science`, even if the save loads.

Also check recipe membership and ownership for advanced circuits, basic circuits, processing units, steel, low-density structures, rocket fuel, matter-related recipes, recycling variants, and any recipes added by the enhancement fork. A load pass is not enough if a technology targets the wrong recipe or appears at the wrong tier.

### 4. Large all-mod stress pass

Use the governed `local-2-1-space-age-mega-smash` root set first:

`aai-industry`, `aai-loaders`, `big-mining-drill`, `biolabs-in-space`, `cargo-ships`, `Cerys-Moon-of-Fulgora`, `corrundum`, `cubium`, `factorioplus`, `FluidMustFlow`, `gleba-reborn`, `Krastorio2-spaced-out`, `kry-inserters`, `lignumis`, `Moshine`, `panglia_planet`, `Paracelsin`, `planet-muluna`, `planet-rabbasca`, `PlutoniumEnergy`, `rubia`, `secretas`, `SimpleSeablock`, and `Small-Space-Age`.

This deliberately stressful combination is useful for discovery, but a failure may belong to interactions among the other mods. When something looks wrong, reproduce it in the smallest ring below before changing MIR.

## Technology And Recipe Review Checklist

For each suspicious or representative technology, record:

- technology ID and displayed name;
- whether it is MIR-generated, a native owner, or an external owner;
- first generated level and maximum useful level;
- prerequisites and when the technology becomes reachable;
- exact science set and which lab accepts it;
- first-level cost, research time, growth formula, and effect per level;
- every target recipe or direct effect;
- whether recipes are missing, duplicated, obsolete, hidden, recycling sinks, or belong to another family;
- icon, description, tree position, arrows, locale fit, and settings discoverability;
- verdict: keep, tune cost, tune effect, move earlier, move later, change science, change membership, or block release.

Prioritize these samples: steel; advanced, electronic, and processing circuits; low-density structures; plastic; rocket fuel; science packs; oil processing and cracking; sulfur and sulfuric acid; modules; mining drills; furnaces; inserters; rails; walls; ammunition; thruster fluids; bioflux and bacteria; molten metals; tungsten; holmium; superconductors; supercapacitors; quantum processors; agriculture growth; spoilage preservation; character reach; cargo landing pads; robot battery; and weapon shooting-speed continuations.

## Tuning Boundaries

Change C15 only for a demonstrated release blocker such as startup failure, invalid targets, wrong ownership, unreachable progression, save or migration loss, severe performance, or clearly unacceptable shipped cost/effect behavior. Package-visible tuning creates a later candidate and invalidates C15-bound evidence; that is expected when playtesting finds a real defect.

Preferences, speculative technologies, broad family generation, provider redesign, and large graph refactors belong after 3.2.0 unless they expose an incorrect shipped contract. Do not promote experimental compiler candidates merely because they look interesting in one modpack.

## Capture An Observation

Use `scripts/New-MIRPlaytestBundle.ps1` for anything we may act on. Example:

```powershell
.\scripts\New-MIRPlaytestBundle.ps1 `
  -CandidateZip .\dist\more-infinite-research_3.2.0.zip `
  -FactorioBin '<Factorio 2.1.11 path>\bin\x64\factorio.exe' `
  -ModsDir '<scenario user-data>\mods' `
  -Category wrong-science `
  -Severity release-blocker `
  -Technology recipe-prod-research_advanced_circuit-1 `
  -Expected 'Red, green, blue, purple, and electromagnetic science' `
  -Actual '<what the technology displayed>' `
  -FactorioLogPath '<scenario user-data>\factorio-current.log'
```

Use the category that best matches the observation: startup, missing or unexpected technology, recipe membership, duplicate owner, prerequisite, science, reachability, timing, effect, cost, icon or locale, settings, save or upgrade, performance, or sanitation review. Add screenshots where they make the tree, tooltip, icon, or settings issue unambiguous. Inspect attachments before sharing a bundle.

For the final release attestation, create the separate exact-candidate packet with `scripts/New-MIRInteractiveReviewPacket.ps1`. A tuning worksheet or playtest bundle does not itself satisfy the release gate.
