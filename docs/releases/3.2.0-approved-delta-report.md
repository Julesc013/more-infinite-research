# 3.2.0 Approved Delta Report

## Outcome

The normalized 3.1.9-to-3.2.0 comparison is approved. The sealed 3.1.9 archive `D77B3A78DA40CD4FDD4C829A01B5030E59FB593F3387124EF5C438F6A9E8DFCD` and the exact 935,025-byte 3.2.0 archive `A856EA9A5E7B63396B6652C5FEBF47F1DF62D2607FF38D4AF8EF9C6E2C4AEF45` were each loaded through seven fresh Factorio 2.1.11 data stages. The artifact binds source commit `1370ca88c06061714e2799ae93c425956b6be0fc` and normalized package-content SHA-256 `02674C2D29FA587781AF2E3B6861793425C1565DC687E0767959C19503DEE115` directly.

The comparison found 200 differences. All 200 are intentional and value-bounded:

- 12 package/version identity rows bind the exact archives;
- 77 rows describe the GenerationPlan and CompilerEvidence contract transition;
- 49 rows describe the compact public coverage report and explicit full internal diagnostic report;
- 60 rows add the reviewed steel stream settings, registry identity, base technology, or Space Age native-owner representation;
- two rows remove only the fixture copper and iron scrap-recovery effects.

No runtime namespace or migration field changed. Technology-ID arrays add exactly one identity per scenario and remove none: `recipe-prod-research_steel-1` for base-derived scenarios or `steel-plate-productivity` for Space Age owner scenarios. The generated base design is fixed at one `+10%` `steel-plate` effect, `8000*2^(L-1)` cost, four reviewed science packs, and 60-second research time. Space Age retains its native `steel-plate-productivity` owner and may adopt only the exact safe fixture recipe.

## Scenario coverage

The exact-package exporter covers:

- base generation;
- Space Age generation;
- automatic-family controls and fixture-authorized generation;
- native-owner adoption;
- default and explicitly enabled base continuations;
- ATAN nuclear-science compatibility;
- Space Age Galore overlapping recipe-productivity ownership.

Base-derived scenarios move from 52 to 53 MIR-owned or MIR-mutated technology identities. Space Age-derived scenarios move from 70 to 71. Every scenario adds one reviewed steel identity and removes none. Six scenarios record one technology-object difference. The native-owner adoption scenario records three: the native steel-owner representation plus the exact copper and iron scrap-recovery exclusions.

## Durable artifacts

`approved-delta/3.1.9-to-3.2.0.json` is the canonical disposition artifact. Every difference records `field`, `before`, `after`, `reason`, `intentional`, `migration_impact`, and `required_evidence`.

`fixtures/export-approved-delta` produces normalized runtime snapshots. `scripts/Export-MIRApprovedDelta.ps1` binds raw local evidence to the exact scenario, archive SHA-256, Factorio version, and producer fingerprint before comparison. Raw snapshots live under `artifacts/approved-delta` and are validation evidence, not release contents.

`scripts/Test-MIRApprovedDelta.ps1` independently rejects unclassified rows, unknown disposition reasons, count drift, removed technology identities, runtime-namespace or migration changes, any steel setting/design value outside the reviewed contract, any scrap effect other than the two named fixture removals, and any scenario outside the seven-row matrix.

## Boundary

This approval establishes the exact intentional difference from 3.1.9. It does not qualify ecosystem breadth, upgrade saves, gameplay, performance scale, protected release evidence, promotion, or backports. Later compiler work must preserve this normalized output unless a new governed delta explicitly approves a change.
