# MIR 3.1.0 Automatic Compiler Roadmap

MIR 3.1.0 owns the foundational automatic-compiler refactor. Work previously labeled 3.2.0 was folded into this unreleased line because only 3.0.5 and 2.3.5 are published baselines. MIR 3.2.0 remains closed until 3.1.0, 2.4.0, and the descending backport ladder are stable and their portable lessons have returned to `dev`.

## Completed Compiler Foundation

- One pure `GenerationPlan` contains fixed streams, structural attachments, reviewed generic families, external adoption, skips, and diagnostic proposals before any generated technology mutation.
- `RecipeFactV2` and shared relationship indexes preserve the evidence required for structural classification, ownership, science/lab compatibility, prerequisite safety, loop denials, complete accounting, and auditable scale counts.
- One effect registry, positive target profiles, declaration-owned setting requirements, schema-2 FamilyRules, and schema-2 CompatibilityPacks drive policy and validation.
- The default `safe-attach` mode covers structurally proven manufacturing without creating incidental technology identities. `safe-generate` can emit only the two predeclared assembler and lab families.
- Pipeline commands declare ordering dependencies; mutation bodies execute through pipeline or emitter transactions and policy remains plan-only.
- Scenario schema 3 records own execution semantics, selection, isolation, and structured assertions. Deterministic planner artifacts support review, and the final Factorio 2.1 manifest contains 89 required scenarios.

## Candidate Closure

1. Keep package-visible source frozen unless a real correctness defect is found.
2. Complete GUI review of settings, locale, icons, technology visibility, science costs, prerequisites, save/load, and balance against the exact tested archive.
3. Acquire and execute complete Angel, Space Exploration, Pyanodon, and representative planet dependency closures where available. Missing archives remain an explicit evidence gap, not an inferred failure or success.
4. Activate one clean candidate record bound to source commit, package-source fingerprint, archive/content hashes, target profile, required groups, scenario manifest, validation harness, upgrade proof, ecosystem campaign, and interactive review.
5. Publish the exact validated bytes without rebuilding, promote the accepted source to `main`, create the real 3.1.0 tag, and freeze the release record.

## Deliberately Deferred From 3.1.0

- Recipe cloning remains a separate `RecipeVariantPlan` design. No recycling-safe duplicate recipe is emitted in 3.1.0.
- Complex chemistry, catalysts, recovery, voiding, transmutation, recycling, probabilistic output, and unsafe multi-output loops remain report-only without stronger graph proof.
- Beacon rules and broad ammunition, armor, battery, circuit, plate, rail, support, and structural-component discovery do not gain speculative automatic families.
- Dedicated machines, private recipe categories, dynamic runtime productivity systems, per-level prototype generation, and hidden-proxy designs remain out of scope.

## Stability Ladder

After 3.1.0 acceptance, derive 2.4.0 from the accepted source with target-declared Factorio 2.0 cuts only. Then qualify 1.9.4, 1.8.2, 1.7.1, 1.6.0, 1.5.0, 1.4.0, and 1.3.0 with matching binaries and target-era schemas. Each target returns portable correctness, determinism, schema, fixture, harness, and diagnostic improvements to `dev`; metadata and feature cuts never return upward.
